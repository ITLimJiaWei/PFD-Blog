PFD Week 1

When i first looked at the challenge statement, "Safe and Seamless Joint Account Creation", I was rather intimidated as I had only worked on account creations for a single person and never two. On top of this, it being safe and seamless was important as the prototype was being designed for OCBC Bank. This means that in theory, security is essential as we would be dealing with OCBC's customers' funds and personal details such as NRIC and more. These requirements made me nervous yet excited to test my skills at something I have never done.
In the first lesson of PFD, our lecturer, Mr Joel Yang, instructed us to fill in our strengths and weakness in terms of technical aspects such as programming knowledge, UI/UX design and more. Later on, we were told to use padlet and fill in our ideas for a prototype which solves the provide challenge statement and vote for other ideas. To my surprise, I was chosen as a Group Leader after receiving the 4th most votes as seen in the image on the left. Following this, I had to select my group members anonymously according to their strengths and weaknesses. Week 1's lesson made me feel rather uneasy as I have never worked with my group members before and was worried that I would not be able to lead a team effectively.
