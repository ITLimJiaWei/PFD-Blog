oneJoint

A Safe and Seamless way to create your Joint Account

Week 1

Figure 1.1 Padlet Ideas

When I first looked at the challenge statement, "Safe and Seamless Joint Account Creation", I was rather intimidated as I had only worked on account creations for a single person and never two. On top of this, it being safe and seamless was important as the prototype was being designed for OCBC Bank. This means that in theory, security is essential as we would be dealing with OCBC's customers' funds and personal details such as NRIC and more. These requirements made me nervous yet excited to test my skills at something I have never done.
In the first lesson of PFD, our lecturer, Mr Joel Yang, instructed us to fill in our strengths and weakness in terms of technical aspects such as programming knowledge, UI/UX design and more. Later on, we were told to use padlet and fill in our ideas for a prototype which solves the provide challenge statement and then vote for other suggestions. To my surprise, I was chosen as a Group Leader after receiving the 4th most votes as seen in the image on the left. Following this, I had to select my group members anonymously according to their strengths and weaknesses. I tried my best to pick members of different strengths that cover each other's weaknesses as well as my own. Week 1's lesson made me feel rather uneasy as I have never worked with my group members before and was worried that I would not be able to lead a team effectively.

Since the chosen challenge statement only involved IT students, I was sure that majority of the team had the necessary technical skills to develop a working prototype for Joint Account Creation. This was due to us undergoing a module called "Web Application Development" in Semester 2.1 which allowed us to develop our full-stack development skills which we had gathered from prior modules such as "Databases" and "Interactive Development". Out of the 5 of us in the team, only 2 members were experienced in Mobile App Development. As a result, we decided on developing a web application using the ASP.NET Core MVC framework and an SQL database. Being the team leader, I set up a Trello Board for us to collaborate and quickly assigned a tasks for each member to complete by an agreed deadline. I also began to work on oneJoint's wireframe at this time on Adobe XD. Since the rest of the team were not adept in UI/UX Design, I took charge of most if not all of the Front End Development.

Week 3

In Week 3, the team and I have completed oneJoint's landing page as seen in Figure 3.1 (Right) and have tested a few 2 factor authentication(2FA) methods to ensure the security of our Joint Account system. These include oneToken, our mobile app authentication method and oneLink, our email authentication method as seen in Figure 3.2 (Below). Jun Hong was in charge of oneToken and created the mobile app for it as well as relevant Firebase libraries and code. Xavier had completed the database script and was modifying it as we discovered more business requirements and bugs with our orignal code. Meanwhile, Tze Siong and Darius worked on polishing our Inception Deck and completing Data Access Layers(DALs) of oneJoint.
Other than Front End Development, I was also responsible for the main flow of the Joint Account application. At first, it was difficult to determine which authentication system to use for what kind of user(E.g. Singaporean/PR, Foreigner etc). However, as I discused with the rest of the team, we had simplified the process to those who were not existing customers to use oneLink and those who were existing customer utilize oneToken due to their existing OCBC accounts which are required to login into our mobile app. Our meetings with our OCBC instructor, Yan Jie were also quite constructive as he had many meaningful insights into what is expected of us and how we should go about achieving it. For example in Week 3, he commented that we had focused too much on business requirements when OCBC Bank was not expecting us to produce a highly accurate Joint Account system.

On week 5 of Assignment 1, we were to participate in the BYTEÂ® Hackz 2021 Hackathon which began on 17/11/2021 9am, and ended on 19/11/2021 around 12.30pm. The first 2 days of the Hackathon were dedicated to bug fixing, implementating last minute features etc. There were also team building games such as nerf and tic tac toe. I was also worried that my UI/UX skills were not sufficient to meet OCBC Bank's high standards and this was coupled with the fact that I was in charge of all the Front End Development of oneJoint.
Due to this anxiety, I started work early in Week 5 on Monday to attempt to fix as many bugs as I could and complete the remaining views before the Hackathon on Wednesday. When Wednesday finally arrived, I felt a sense of despair due to the fact that I had not finished all of oneJoint's views and its back end code. Thankfully, Jun Hong and Xavier assisted me with the back end code while I completed the remaining views as seen in Figure 5.1 and 5.2 (Above) while we discussed other problems with the oneJoint prototype. Furthermore, we came together as team when we strived to win the nerf challenge organized by Overflow SIG during the hackathon. With the help of my teammates and the organized activities, I felt more at ease.

Day 2 of the Hackathon began and the whole team was struggling to fix the multiple bugs that oneJoint still had. In hindsight, this could have been avoided if we had adopted Test Driven Development(TDD) but unfortunately we were only aware of it a few weeks after the Hackathon. Relating to our lack of knowledge for TDD, I felt that our skills in Agile Methodology were lacking largely due to the fact that by Week 5, for our respective SDD classes, we had only come up with an inception deck and have not actually practised the software development methodlogy in a real project as of yet. Throughout these 2 days, Jun Hong surprised me with the amount of effort/time he was willing to put in to deliver a good prototype/presentation to OCBC Bank. Not only was he responsible for oneJoint's 2FA methods but also assisted me in back end code and database as well.
However, our team had not completed our presentation slides. The planned video showcasing a typical joint application was not recorded and numerous slides were still incomplete. After going through our rough script with Mr Joel Yang late in the Afternoon on Day 2 of the Hackathon, I rushed home to edit the recorded demo as previously mentioned which was recorded by Jun Hong on the last day. Being inexperienced with Adobe Premiere Pro and professional editing software, I turned to iMovie on my iPhone and succesfully edited and compiled the recording in around 2 hours. My anxiety had returned and I believed all my teammates and I were extremely fatigued from the long day but still determined to put out a good presentation tomorrow. As a result, Jun Hong, Darius and I stayed up to 2am that night to rehearse and polish our presentation prior to OCBC Bank reviewing it for themselves. During the day of presentation, I felt confident in oneJoint and my team and I managed to deliver the presentation as planned. However, I still believed we could have done much better.

Post Assignment 1 Reflection

Even as I write this blog today, I can stil recall the numerous hours I had put into oneJoint. Despite this, I believe several aspects of how I handled my team and oneJoint could have been much better. For example, I usually was the one to initiate group discussions early on in the Assignment which led to many of my ideas being implemented and as a result, my teammates did not have as much of a influence in oneJoint. I should have pushed for my teammates to offer their ideas as I definitely would not have the best solutions to all the problems we would encounter during these 5 weeks. Secondly, during the presentation, one of the OCBC judges suggested that we should have had a SMS one time password system alongside our existing oneLink and oneToken authentication methods. This was one of our cut features due to a lack of time management and we intend to correct this flaw in Assignemnt 2 in January. I came into PFD confident to experience proper software development to deliver a product of high quality. Although I believe that we had put out a decent product, it was not the one I had expected at the start of this module. However, I did pick up many leadership skills and now have a greater understanding of how to prioritize and delegate tasks to others. Going forward, it is unlikely I will ever forget oneJoint due to the stress, anxiety it caused and yet I look forward to improving it in Assignment 2!
