oneJoint

A Safe and Seamless way to create your Joint Account

Week 1

Figure 1.1 Padlet Ideas

When I first looked at the challenge statement, "Safe and Seamless Joint Account Creation", I was rather intimidated as I had only worked on account creations for a single person and never two. On top of this, it being safe and seamless was important as the prototype was being designed for OCBC Bank. This means that in theory, security is essential as we would be dealing with OCBC's customers' funds and personal details such as NRIC and more. These requirements made me nervous yet excited to test my skills at something I have never done.
In the first lesson of PFD, our lecturer, Mr Joel Yang, instructed us to fill in our strengths and weakness in terms of technical aspects such as programming knowledge, UI/UX design and more. Later on, we were told to use padlet and fill in our ideas for a prototype which solves the provide challenge statement and then vote for other suggestions. To my surprise, I was chosen as a Group Leader after receiving the 4th most votes as seen in the image on the left. Following this, I had to select my group members anonymously according to their strengths and weaknesses. I tried my best to pick members of different strengths that cover each other's weaknesses as well as my own. Week 1's lesson made me feel rather uneasy as I have never worked with my group members before and was worried that I would not be able to lead a team effectively.

Since the chosen challenge statement only involved IT students, I was sure that majority of the team had the necessary technical skills to develop a working prototype for Joint Account Creation. This was due to us undergoing a module called "Web Application Development" in Semester 2.1 which allowed us to develop our full-stack development skills which we had gathered from prior modules such as "Databases" and "Interactive Development". Out of the 5 of us in the team, only 2 members were experienced in Mobile App Development. As a result, we decided on developing a web application using the ASP.NET Core MVC framework and an SQL database. Being the team leader, I set up a Trello Board for us to collaborate and quickly assigned a tasks for each member to complete by an agreed deadline. I also began to work on oneJoint's wireframe at this time on Adobe XD. Since the rest of the team were not adept in UI/UX Design, I took charge of most if not all of the Front End Development.

Week 2

Figure 2.2 Group Conflict Management Styles

Once I had settled in with my new group members, Week 2's Lesson had begun. Mr Joel Yang went on to brief us on group/conflict management and that there were many different conflict management styles. As seen from Figure 2.1 (Above), there were 5 styles consisting of a shark, turtle, fox, teddy-bear and owl which all have their own characteristics. After completing some questions, my team and I were able to determine which animal we were able to relate to the most. I was quite well rounded excluding the turtle with Collaborating and Accommodating being my highest triats at 9 which means I am most like a teddy-bear and an owl. The rest of my team also had similar traits as seen in Figure 2.2 (Above) which made me believe we would be able to work together well to deliver a good prototype for OCBC bank.
My team and I were currently in the storming/norming stages of team development where some are afraid to show their opinion and there could be some clashes in opinion on how to proceed. Thankfully, we were respectful towards one another and managed to agree on certain issues early on so they do not persist and drag down our productivity later on.

Week 3

In Week 3, the team and I have completed oneJoint's landing page as seen in Figure 3.1 (Right) and have tested a few 2 factor authentication(2FA) methods to ensure the security of our Joint Account system. These include oneToken, our mobile app authentication method and oneLink, our email authentication method as seen in Figure 3.2 (Below). Jun Hong was in charge of oneToken and created the mobile app for it as well as relevant Firebase libraries and code. Xavier had completed the database script and was modifying it as we discovered more business requirements and bugs with our orignal code. Meanwhile, Tze Siong and Darius worked on polishing our Inception Deck and completing Data Access Layers(DALs) of oneJoint.
Other than Front End Development, I was also responsible for the main flow of the Joint Account application. At first, it was difficult to determine which authentication system to use for what kind of user(E.g. Singaporean/PR, Foreigner etc). However, as I discused with the rest of the team, we had simplified the process to those who were not existing customers to use oneLink and those who were existing customer utilize oneToken due to their existing OCBC accounts which are required to login into our mobile app. Our meetings with our OCBC instructor, Yan Jie were also quite constructive as he had many meaningful insights into what is expected of us and how we should go about achieving it. For example in Week 3, he commented that we had focused too much on business requirements when OCBC Bank was not expecting us to produce a highly accurate Joint Account system.
